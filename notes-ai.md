# John Evans — Blueprint to a 40" Vertical

**Coach background:** Coached 4 of the 6 athletes who have tested a 50" vertical. Hundreds of athletes over 40". Pioneered individualized progressive vertical jump training.

---

## Core Principles

- Progressive overload
- Variety & individuality
- Rest and recovery
- **Fatigue/Fitness model:** higher training → higher fitness AND higher fatigue → performance dips temporarily. Macro cycles exploit this by stacking adaptations.
- **Specificity balance:** as specific days get more specific, general days must get more general (and vice versa). Too specific = injury.

---

## Structure: 6-Month Macro Cycle → 6 Mesocycles

Each mesocycle ~1 month (3 weeks of work + 1 deload on week 4 in Month 1).

---

## Mesocycle 1 — Volume Building (Month 1)

**Goal:** Build base volume with general work.

**Lifting (Mon/Wed/Fri):**
- 3 days/week
- Power exercise: **5 sets × 4 reps** (builds volume to intensify later)
- Squats: **4–5 sets × 6–12 reps @ 65–70%**
- Accessories: **8–12 reps** (up to 14 for fit athletes)
- Deload on **week 4**

**Exercise selection (general work — mimics jumping less):**
- 2-foot jumpers: sumo deadlifts, unilateral work
- 1-foot jumpers: deep squats, full ROM movements

**General days (Tue/Thu):**
- Variety of movements to build overall athleticism ("Bruce Wayne → Batman")

---

## Mesocycle 2 — Intensification / Specific Work (Month 2)

**Goal:** Increase specificity and intensity; prime Month 3.

**Lifting (Mon/Wed/Fri):**
- Power work → move to **Olympic lifts** (2-foot jumpers):
  - Clean pulls: **6 sets × 3–5 reps @ ~90%** of power clean
  - Power cleans: **6 sets × 3–5 reps @ 75–85%**
  - Sometimes snatch pulls @ ~90%
  - Sets: start low end (5), increase per week up to 7
- Max strength: **6–8 rep range** (follow power work)
- Accessories: more specific, primes next cycle
  - Drop reps down (e.g. RDLs, calf raises → **4–6 reps @ up to 85%**)

**General days (Tue/Thu):**
- Add slight specificity here so Mon/Wed/Fri doesn't need to be overly specific
- Bodybuilding circuits (lower + upper body)
- **Tempo running** (improves body composition + cardiorespiratory system)

---

## Mesocycle 3 — Absolute Strength (Month 3, ~3 weeks)

**Goal:** Peak strength; prime eccentric strength for Month 4.

**Lifting (Mon/Wed/Fri):**
- Back squat: **10 sets × 2–3 reps** (near max)
- Clean pulls: **105–110% of power clean max** (builds first/second pull strength; makes power cleans feel easier)
- Power cleans: push percentages up significantly
- Accessories: **eccentric strength** (primes Month 4 main lifts)
  - Nordic hamstring curls
  - 2-up/1-down calf raises

**General days (Tue/Thu):**
- **Jump circuits in sand:** 20–30 sec, variety of jumps (toe touch, tuck jumps, single-leg spins)
- **Tempo runs:** 30–45 sec runs @ conversational effort, **~3 min rest** — increase reps/sets vs. Month 2; keep legs sub-lactic

---

## Mesocycle 4 — Strength-Speed / Eccentric Emphasis (Month 4)

**Goal:** Move bars fast with heavy loads; develop eccentric strength and triple extension.

**Lifting (Mon/Wed/Fri):**
- Power cleans: **2×/week, push to absolute maxes** (strong from clean pull work)
- Clean pull day on 3rd lifting day
- **Triple extension work** (hip/knee/ankle):
  - Eccentric split squats: 3 build-up sets normal, then **3 working sets × 2–3 reps supramaximal** (safety bar, hold rack, hands-assist up)
  - Belt squat accentuated eccentrics: **110–115% of back squat max** (partner assists concentric, athlete lowers)
- Partial ROM work:
  - 1-foot jumpers: pull from blocks
  - 2-foot jumpers: pull from floor, full ROM
- Accessories (elastic qualities): **2 sets × 5–6 reps** only (higher volume → neural fatigue)
  - High-velocity calf raises, bouncing calf raises
  - Drop RDLs (drop fast, stand explosively)

**General days (Tue/Thu):**
- Return to general: bodyweight circuits, timed bodybuilding circuits
- Remove tempo runs (joint/tendon recovery)
- Remove jump circuits
- Focus: recovery + maintaining fitness base

---

## Mesocycle 5 — Speed-Strength / Bar Velocity (Month 5)

**Goal:** Convert strength to speed; move from strength-speed → speed-strength.

**Lifting (Mon/Wed/Fri):**
- Olympic lifts: **lighter and faster**
  - Power snatch: ~50% of power clean max
  - Power cleans: **60–70%** (fast!)
  - Hang power cleans (builds SSC into Oly lifts)
- Squats: blend max strength + high velocity
  - Primer sets: **5→4→3 reps, ascending to ~85%**
  - Then high-velocity squats: **65–70%, timed set of 5** (as fast as possible)
  - Avoid doing both heavy and fast squats together — neural fry risk
- Accessories: prime Month 6 (shock loading)
  - Med ball throws: overhead back, between-legs forward, overhead back
  - Shot throws
  - Low plyometric volumes: box step-offs, step-up jumps (high variety)

**General days (Tue/Thu):**
- Even more general than Month 4 (specific work is very specific now)
- Static flexibility, light bodyweight circuits, core work
- **No jumping on general days**
- Optional: barefoot 100m run × 10 (walk back rest)

---

## Mesocycle 6 — Maximum Specificity (Month 6)

**Goal:** Peak for testing; highest specificity and plyometric volume.

**Option A — Load Management:**
- Jump days: Mon + Thu
- "Feel good" lifting: Tue + Fri (slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs — very easy)

**Option B — Integrated (preferred):**
- Mon/Wed/Fri: sprints + heavy plyometric volume (intensity + variety, not necessarily high reps)
- Plyos: used most heavily here across the whole year

**Accessories:**
- Flip back to general/non-specific (to protect nervous system)
- Concentric-only: regular RDLs, slow calf raises
- Keeps athletes healthy and CNS fresh

---

## Taper (Post Macro Cycle)

- **Cut volume ~50%, increase intensity**
- Max effort jumping more frequently
- Maintain (don't drop) general work volume
- Squat: **5→4→3→2→1 protocol**
- Power clean: daily max for velocity

---

## Jump Sessions (Year-Round Progression)

Total session window: **40 minutes**

| Mesocycle | Low-Effort Warm-Up | Max Effort |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| Month 1   | 35 min            | 5 min      |
| Month 2   | 30 min            | 10 min     |
| Month 3   | 25 min            | 15 min     |
| Month 4+  | Cap at **20/20**  | 20 min     |

- Don't exceed 20 min max effort — leads to flatness and poor performance on lifting days.

---

## Injury Prevention Notes

- Never let specific days AND general days be highly specific simultaneously → injury
- Eccentric and supramaximal work requires careful volume management
- Plyometric volume stays **low all year** except Month 6
- High-velocity accessory work capped at **2 sets** to avoid neural fatigue
- Tempo runs kept sub-lactic (uncomfortable but not painful)
- Hired DPT on staff for complex injuries (ACL, PCL, meniscus, shoulders)
