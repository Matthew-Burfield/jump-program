# Vertical Jump Guiding Principles

### 1. The 10% Rule (Auto-regulation)

If during your CNS Primers (the 2 max-effort jumps) your performance feels or measures more than 10% below your recent best, **cut the session volume by 50%.** Do not grind through "heavy legs."

### 2. Intent Over Load

In jumping, moving a light weight fast is often superior to moving a heavy weight slowly. Every concentric (upward) phase of a lift must be performed with **maximal violent intent**, regardless of the weight on the bar.

### 3. The "Freshness" Priority

The Central Nervous System (CNS) is the driver of the vertical jump. You cannot train the CNS effectively while it is fatigued. Always perform jumps and sprints **before** lifting.

### 4. Respect the Tempo

In the Accumulation Phase, the 4-second eccentric (descent) and 3-second isometric (hold) are non-negotiable. These are not "suggestions"; they are the mechanical stress required to remodel your tendons.

### 5. Quality over Quantity

A vertical jump is a 0.2-second explosion. Doing 50 mediocre jumps is worse than doing 5 perfect ones. If the "pop" is gone, the workout is over.
