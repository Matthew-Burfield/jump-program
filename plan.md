# 6-Month Vertical Jump Macrocycle — John Evans Framework

**Baseline (start of macrocycle):**
- Bodyweight: ~100kg
- Back squat est. 1RM: ~165kg
- Power clean est. max: ~110kg
- CMJ: ~24" | Approach: ~26.5"
- Jumper type: 2-foot (adjust exercise selection notes if this changes)

**Weekly structure:** Sun = jump session · Mon/Wed/Fri = specific lifting days · Tue/Thu = general days · Sat = rest

---

## Jump Sessions — Full Program Guide

Jump sessions happen **once per week on Sunday**, as a standalone session the day before the Monday lift. Evans flags that overdoing the jump session makes the Monday lifting session flat — Sunday gives a buffer without a full rest day in between.

### Structure: 40-minute total window

The 40 minutes is split between low-effort warm-up jumping and max-effort jumping. The split shifts across the macrocycle:

| Month | Low-effort warm-up | Max effort | Est. max attempts |
|-------|--------------------|------------|-------------------|
| 1 | 35 min | 5 min | 3–4 |
| 2 | 30 min | 10 min | 6–8 |
| 3 | 25 min | 15 min | 10–12 |
| 4–6 | 20 min | 20 min | 12–15 |

Cap the max effort block at 20 minutes — going beyond this leads to flatness and kills the lifting session that follows.

### What is "low-effort warm-up jumping"?

This is NOT approach jumps. It's graduated movement that prepares the body without fatiguing it:
- Ankle bounces / pogos
- Skips and bounds
- Submaximal CMJs (60–70% effort)
- Approach run-throughs without jumping — practice the steps and penultimate
- Submaximal approach jumps at 75–80% effort (touching the backboard comfortably, not reaching)
- Stutter steps, hip-open runs

The goal is to accumulate movement volume, groove the approach pattern, and bring the CNS online. You should not be gassing yourself. If you're breathing hard, you're going too hard.

### What is "max effort jumping"?

Full approach jumps at 100% intent — these are your dunking attempts, backboard reach tests, or whatever your max-height measure is. Treat each one like a test rep:
- Full rest between attempts: **60–90 seconds minimum** in Months 1–3, **90–120 seconds** in Months 4–6 as intensity peaks
- Quality over quantity — if the pop drops off noticeably, stop the block early
- **CNS primer check:** Before the max effort block, do 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab). If either reads >10% below your recent best, skip the max effort block entirely and keep the session as warm-up only. Do not grind through flat legs.

### Month-by-month intent

**Month 1 (5 min max effort = ~3–4 attempts):** You're not trying to set a PR. You're just practising max intent after a long warm-up. The ratio is deliberately conservative — tendons and joints are still adapting from Phase 1.

**Month 2 (10 min = ~6–8 attempts):** Starting to build a real jump practice. Still plenty of warm-up, but now you have enough max reps to get sharp.

**Month 3 (15 min = ~10–12 attempts):** You'll start seeing real jump progress here. The strength base from the lifting is beginning to express itself.

**Month 4–6 (20 min = 12–15 attempts):** This is where the jump session becomes a genuine training stimulus, not just a warm-up adjunct. Rest fully. Every attempt counts.

### Taper exception

During the taper (post macrocycle), bump to **2× jump sessions per week** — Evans explicitly says "max effort jumping more frequently." This is the only time frequency increases. The 40-minute structure still applies.

## Month 1 — Volume Building

**Goal:** Build general work capacity. Tendons and joints adapt to load. No specificity yet.
**Deload:** Week 4 — cut all volumes 50%, maintain intensity.

### Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

**Power exercise — 5 sets × 4 reps**
Start with a bilateral jump-pattern movement. Options: box jump, broad jump, or trap bar jump squat at 20%.
*This is volume, not max effort. Clean, powerful reps.*

**Back Squat — 4–5 sets × 6–12 reps @ 65–70% (full ROM, controlled)**

| Week | Sets × Reps | Load |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | 4 × 10 | 107.5kg (65%) |
| 2 | 4 × 8 | 112.5kg (68%) |
| 3 | 5 × 6 | 115kg (70%) |
| 4 (Deload) | 2 × 8 | 107.5kg (65%) |

**Accessory work — 8–12 reps (up to 14 if fitness allows)**
As 2-foot jumper, lean general/bilateral:
- Sumo deadlift: 3–4 sets × 8–10
- Leg press or hack squat: 3 sets × 10–12
- Hamstring curl (machine): 3 sets × 10–12
- Calf raise (slow, full ROM): 3 sets × 12
- Core: weighted carries, ab wheel, Copenhagen planks

### General Days (Tue / Thu)

Goal: build athleticism, don't replicate the lifting days.
- Upper body push/pull (bench, rows, pull-ups, dips)
- Bodyweight lower circuits (lunges, step-ups, glute bridges)
- Tibialis raises, band work, hip activation
- Light tempo run: 6–8 × 100m @ ~70% effort, walk back rest
- Total session: ~45–60 min

## Month 2 — Intensification / Specific Work

**Goal:** Increase intensity and move toward Olympic lifts. Prime Month 3 via accessory specificity.

### Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

**Power work — Olympic lifts (2-foot jumpers)**

*Clean pulls:* 6 sets × 3–5 reps @ **90% of power clean max (~100kg)**
*Power cleans:* 6 sets × 3–5 reps @ **75–85% (~82–93kg)**
- Start at 5 sets, add 1 set per week (5 → 6 → 7)
- Start at 4 reps/set, drop to 3 reps/set as sets increase

| Week | Clean Pull | Power Clean |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | 5 × 4 @ 100kg | 5 × 4 @ 82kg |
| 2 | 6 × 3 @ 100kg | 6 × 3 @ 87kg |
| 3 | 7 × 3 @ 100kg | 7 × 3 @ 93kg |
| 4 (Deload) | 4 × 3 @ 90kg | 4 × 3 @ 80kg |

**Max strength — follow power work**
Back squat or front squat: **4–5 sets × 6–8 reps**

| Week | Sets × Reps | Load |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | 4 × 8 | 120kg (~73%) |
| 2 | 4 × 6 | 127.5kg (~77%) |
| 3 | 5 × 6 | 132.5kg (~80%) |
| 4 (Deload) | 3 × 6 | 115kg |

**Accessories — start priming eccentric strength (Month 3 primer)**
- RDL: 3 sets × 4–6 reps @ 80–85% — drop reps down from Month 1, increase load
- Calf raise (single leg, slow): 3 sets × 6 @ heavy
- Nordic hamstring curl: 3 sets × 5–6 (begin introducing)
- Keep reps low, intensity high vs. Month 1

### General Days (Tue / Thu)

Slightly more specific than Month 1 — Evans' principle: small specificity on general days so lifting days don't need to be overly specific.
- Bodybuilding circuits (quads, hamstrings, glutes, upper body)
- Tempo running: **8–10 × 100m @ 70–75%**, walk back — keep legs sub-lactic, not painful
- No jump circuits yet

## Month 3 — Absolute Strength (~3 weeks + deload)

**Goal:** Peak strength. This is the heaviest month. Sets are unusually high. Accessories prime eccentric work for Month 4.

### Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

**Clean pulls — heavy**
105–110% of power clean max = **115–120kg**

| Week | Sets × Reps | Load |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | 6 × 3 | 115kg |
| 2 | 7 × 3 | 117.5kg |
| 3 | 8 × 3 | 120kg |
| 4 (Deload) | 4 × 3 | 105kg |

*Rationale: builds first-pull strength and teaches explosive second pull. Power cleans will feel light after this.*

**Back Squat — 10 sets × 2–3 reps (near max)**

| Week | Sets × Reps | Load |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | 8 × 3 | 137.5kg (~83%) |
| 2 | 10 × 3 | 142.5kg (~86%) |
| 3 | 10 × 2 | 150kg (~91%) |
| 4 (Deload) | 5 × 2 | 130kg |

**Accessories — eccentric primer for Month 4**
- Nordic hamstring curl: 3 sets × 5 (full effort, control the descent)
- 2-up / 1-down calf raise: 3 sets × 6 each leg
- These are priming Month 4's main eccentric emphasis — treat them seriously

### General Days (Tue / Thu)

- **Jump circuits in sand:** 20–30 sec per circuit, variety of jumps (toe touch jumps, tuck jumps, single-leg hops, spins). Rest fully between.
- **Tempo runs:** 30–45 sec @ controlled effort, **3 min rest** — increase reps/sets vs Month 2. Sub-lactic only.
- No heavy lower body gym work on these days

---

## Month 4 — Strength-Speed / Eccentric Emphasis

**Goal:** Move bars fast with heavy loads. Develop eccentric strength and triple extension. General days get easier as lifting days get more specific.

### Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

**Power cleans — 2×/week, push to absolute maxes**
Strong from the clean pull work — now cash it in.
- Day 1 + Day 2: Power cleans working up to daily max
- Day 3: Clean pull @ 105–110%

**Triple extension work — eccentric emphasis**

*Eccentric split squats (safety bar, hold rack):*
- 3 build-up sets × 5 reps (normal tempo, moderate load)
- 3 working sets × 2–3 reps supramaximal (hands assist concentric, full eccentric)
- Load: slightly above normal split squat max

*Rack accentuated eccentrics (belt squat substitute):*
- Set safety bars just below parallel depth
- Load **110–115% of back squat max = ~182–190kg** on the barbell
- Lower under full control to the safeties, let the bar settle, step out and re-rack
- Eccentric only — no concentric. Un-rack fresh each rep.
- Sets: 4–5 × 3 reps
- *This is the primary eccentric stimulus for patellar tendon and quad*

*Partial ROM / block pulls (2-foot jumpers: full ROM from floor)*

**Accessories — elastic / high velocity (keep volume low)**
*Neural fatigue accumulates fast here — 2 sets maximum.*
- High-velocity calf raises: 2 × 6 (fast as possible)
- Drop RDL (drop down fast, drive up explosively): 2 × 5
- Bouncing calf raises: 2 × 8

### General Days (Tue / Thu)

Get *more general* — lifting days are the most specific they've been.
- Bodyweight circuits (no loaded lower body)
- Upper body: bench, rows, pull-ups
- **Remove tempo runs** — tendons and joints need the recovery
- **Remove jump circuits** — save the legs for lifting days
- Optional: light core, hip mobility, static stretching

---

## Month 5 — Speed-Strength / Bar Velocity

**Goal:** Convert strength to speed. Move from strength-speed → speed-strength. Bars move fast and light.

### Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

**Olympic lifts — fast and light**
- Power snatch: **~50% of power clean max = ~55kg**, 5–6 sets × 3 reps — move it fast
- Power cleans: **60–70% = ~66–77kg**, 5–6 sets × 3 reps — bar speed is the metric
- Hang power cleans: builds SSC into the lift — 3–4 sets × 3 @ 65%

| Exercise | Load | Target |
|----------|------|--------|
| Power snatch | ~55kg | Explosive, bar speed priority |
| Power clean | 66–77kg | Sharp, fast — not grinding |
| Hang power clean | ~70kg | SSC emphasis |

**Squats — blend max strength + high velocity**
Do NOT do both at max intensity — neural fry risk.

*Primer (ascending):* 5 reps → 4 reps → 3 reps, increase load up to ~85% (~140kg)
*Then high-velocity set:* 5 reps @ 65–70% (~107–115kg), timed — move it as fast as possible

**Accessories — prime Month 6 (shock loading)**
- Med ball throws: overhead back, between-legs forward, overhead scoop — 3 sets × 5
- Step-off box (low box, land and hold): 3 × 5 — introduce reactive landing
- Step-up jumps: 3 × 5 — low plyometric volume, high variety
*Keep plyometric volume low — Month 6 is where it peaks*

### General Days (Tue / Thu)

Most general they've been — specific work is at its highest so far.
- Static flexibility / mobility
- Light bodyweight circuits or core
- **No jumping on general days**
- Optional: barefoot 100m × 10, walk back (aerobic maintenance only)

---

## Month 6 — Maximum Specificity

**Goal:** Peak expression. Highest specificity and plyometric intensity. Weight room becomes maintenance.

### Option A — Load Management (recommended if fatigued)

- **Sun:** Jump session (approach jumps, dunking, max effort) — same 40-min structure
- **Mon + Wed:** "Feel good" lifts — slow squats, slow calf raises, RDLs. Very easy, no DOMS.
- Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat: rest or light activity

### Option B — Integrated (if recovered well)

**Mon / Wed / Fri:**
- Sprints: 4–6 × 30–40m @ 90–95% — full rest between
- Plyometrics: depth jumps, approach jumps, reactive bounds — intensity and variety high, volume moderate
- Jump session (20/20 split — see table above)

**Accessories (flip back to general):**
- Slow, concentric-focused: regular RDLs, slow calf raises (3 sets × 8)
- Keeps joints healthy and CNS fresh for jumping

**General Days (Tue / Thu):**
- Very easy. Mobility, upper body, walks.
- Absolutely no lower body loading that competes with the specific work.

---

## Taper (Post Macrocycle — ~1–2 weeks)

**Cut volume ~50%. Increase intensity. Jump frequently.**

- **Squats:** 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1 protocol, ascending to heavy single
- **Power clean:** Daily max for velocity — not grinding a PR, chasing bar speed
- **General work:** Maintain but keep volume low — don't drop it entirely
- **Jumping:** Max effort more frequently, full rest between attempts
- No new stimulus. No DOMS going into the test.

**Test day:**
- Morning, fasted bodyweight
- Full warm-up (pogos, approach run-throughs, build to 2–3 near-max jumps)
- 3–5 max effort approach jumps, full rest between each
- Record peak

---

## Key Principles Across All 6 Months

1. **Specificity balance:** as lifting days get more specific, general days must get more general — never run both at high specificity simultaneously
2. **CNS primers first:** 2× CMJ measured before every session. 10% drop = 50% volume cut
3. **Accessories potentiate the next cycle** — they're not junk volume, they're primers
4. **Plyometric volume stays low all year** except Month 6
5. **High-velocity accessory work:** cap at 2 sets — neural fatigue accumulates fast
6. **Tempo runs stay sub-lactic** — uncomfortable but never painful legs
7. **Copenhagen planks in warm-up every session** — non-negotiable given groin history
