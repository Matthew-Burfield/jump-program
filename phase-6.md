# Phase 6: Maximum Specificity (Weeks 25–28)

**Goal:** Peak expression. Highest specificity and plyometric intensity of the macrocycle. The
weight room becomes maintenance — jumping is the training stimulus.
**Stimulus:** Approach jumps, sprints, depth jumps, reactive bounds. Lifting keeps the body primed
but does not compete with the specific work.
**Frequency:** Determined by recovery — choose Option A or B honestly.
**Deload / Taper:** Week 28 transitions into the taper

---

## Sunday — Jump Session

**Total window: 40 minutes**

**Low-effort warm-up (20 min):**
Pogos, skips, bounds, submaximal CMJs (60–70%), approach run-throughs, submaximal approach jumps
at 75–80%.

**Max effort (20 min):**
~12–15 full approach jumps at 100% intent.

**Before the max effort block — CNS primer check:**
- 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab)
- If either reads >10% below recent best → skip the max effort block entirely, keep as warm-up only

**Max effort rest:** 90–120 seconds between attempts. Full rest — every attempt counts.

---

## Choose Based on Recovery

### Option A — Load Management *(if fatigued going in)*

*Use this if Phase 5 left accumulated fatigue. Jumping is the primary training stimulus.*

- **Sunday:** Jump session (full 40-min structure above)
- **Mon + Wed:** "Feel good" lifts only — slow tempo squats, slow calf raises, RDLs.
  These should not cause DOMS. If you're sore after, you went too heavy.
- **Tue / Thu / Fri / Sat:** Rest or very light activity (walks, stretching)

### Option B — Integrated *(if recovered well going in)*

*Use this if you've been recovering well and Phase 5 left no residual fatigue.*

**Mon / Wed / Fri:**

*Sprints:* 4–6 × 30–40m @ 90–95% effort. Full rest between reps (2–3 min). These are approach
quality — explosive off the line.

*Plyometrics:* Depth jumps, approach jumps, reactive bounds. Intensity and variety high, volume
moderate (total contacts: 30–50 per session max).

*Accessories (maintenance only — flip back to general):*
- Slow concentric-focused: RDLs, slow calf raises — 3 sets × 8 each
- Keeps joints healthy and CNS fresh for jumping without adding fatigue

**General Days (Tue / Thu):**
- Very easy: mobility, upper body (bench, rows, pull-ups), walks
- **No lower body loading** that competes with the specific work

---

## Week 28 — Transition to Taper

The last week of Phase 6 feeds directly into the taper:
- Cut jump session warm-up reps slightly (keep the 20-min max effort window intact)
- Lifting: maintenance only — do not add load or volume
- Arrive at the taper fresh — begin reducing overall session RPE

---

## Taper (Post Week 28 — ~1–2 weeks)

**Goal:** Peak for test day. No new stimulus. No DOMS going into the test.

**Cut volume ~50%. Increase relative intensity. Jump more frequently.**

**Squats:** 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1 protocol, ascending to a heavy single. Stop when a rep slows —
do not grind.

**Power clean:** Daily max for velocity — chasing bar speed, not a PR grind.

**General work:** Maintain but keep volume low. Don't drop it entirely.

**Jumping:** Bump to **2× jump sessions per week** — this is the only time in the macrocycle that
frequency increases. Same 40-min structure applies.

**Test day:**
- Morning, fasted bodyweight
- Full warm-up: pogos, approach run-throughs, build to 2–3 near-max jumps
- 3–5 max effort approach jumps, full rest between each
- Record peak

---

## Warm-Up (Every Session)

**Copenhagen planks: 3 × 20–30s each side** — non-negotiable, every session.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Option A vs B: be honest about fatigue** — underrecovery here costs test-day performance
- **No new stimulus from Week 28 onward** — adaptations are locked in, just peak
- **Taper jumps 2×/week** — the only time in the program frequency increases
- **Test day: no heavy lifting in the final week** — do not sneak it in
