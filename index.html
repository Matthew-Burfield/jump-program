Guiding Principles

Core rules that override all programming decisions

1
The 10% Rule (Auto-regulation)
If CNS primer jumps read >10% below your recent best → cut all working sets by 50%. Do not grind through heavy legs.
2
Intent Over Load
Moving a light weight fast is often superior to moving a heavy weight slowly. Every concentric phase must be performed with maximal violent intent regardless of weight.
3
Freshness Priority
The CNS drives the vertical jump. You cannot train it while fatigued. Always perform jumps and sprints before lifting — no exceptions.
4
Respect the Tempo
Phase 0's 4s eccentric and 3s isometric are not suggestions. They are the mechanical stress required to remodel tendons. No shortcuts.
5
Quality Over Quantity
A vertical jump is a 0.2-second explosion. 5 perfect jumps beats 50 mediocre ones. If the "pop" is gone, the session is over.
6
Specificity Balance
As lifting days get more specific, general days must get more general. Never run both at high specificity simultaneously — that's how injuries happen.
7
Copenhagen Planks Every Session
Non-negotiable warm-up item. 3 × 20–30s each side. Do not skip regardless of time pressure.
