# Phase 2: Intensification (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** Increase intensity and prime absolute strength. Phase 2 moves up the force-velocity curve — power output increases, reps drop, loads climb. Olympic lifting replaces the jump squat as the explosive stimulus.
**Stimulus:** Olympic lifting (power cleans + clean pulls), higher-intensity squats, eccentric accessories.
**Tempo:** Standard controlled tempo — no strict counts. Eccentric on RDL is deliberate but not timed.
**Frequency:** 3 lifting days (Mon/Wed/Fri) + 1 jump session (Sun)
**Deload:** Week 12 (Week 4 of this phase)

---

## Sunday — Jump Session

*Same structure as Phase 1, but the max-effort window expands. More max attempts = more specificity.*

**Total window: 40 minutes**

**Low-effort warm-up (30 min):**
Pogos, skips, bounds, submaximal CMJs (60–70%), approach run-throughs, submaximal approach jumps
at 75–80%.

**Max effort (10 min):**
~6–8 full approach jumps at 100% intent.

**Before the max effort block — CNS primer check:**
- 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab)
- If either reads >10% below recent best → skip the max effort block entirely, keep as warm-up only

**Max effort rest:** 60–90 seconds between attempts. If the pop drops off, stop early.

*You have more max attempts now — don't rush through them. Each rep is a quality rep.*

---

## Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

### CNS Primers
*Always first, before any lifting.*

**Standing CMJ × 2 reps — measured via My Jump Lab**
- Max intent every rep, full reset between jumps
- 3 min full rest between jumps
- **If either jump reads >10% below recent best → cut all working sets by 50% today**

---

### Olympic Lift
*Power exercise — always before squats. This replaces the trap bar jump squat from Phase 1.*

**Monday / Friday — Power Clean**

*Full triple extension. Catch in the power position — no squat under. Reset between reps.
Rest 2–3 min between sets.*

| Week        | Sets × Reps | Load              |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 9           | 5 × 4       | 82.5kg (~75%)     |
| 10          | 6 × 3       | 87.5kg (~80%)     |
| 11          | 7 × 3       | 92.5kg (~85%)     |

**Wednesday — Clean Pull**

*Drive through the floor, full extension at the top — no catch. Heavier than the power clean.
The pull reinforces position and builds first-pull strength that feeds back into the clean.*

## General Days (Tue / Thu)

Upper body lifting first (see `upper-body.md`), then tempo runs.

**Tempo Runs: 8–10 × 100m @ 70–75% effort**
- Walk back as rest between reps
- Keep it sub-lactic — uncomfortable but never painful legs
- Sprint work from Phase 0/1 is retired; this is aerobic conditioning, not acceleration work

*Slightly more specific than Phase 1 general days — Evans' principle: small specificity on general
days so lifting days don't need to carry the full load.*

---

## Warm-Up (Every Session)

**Copenhagen planks: 3 × 20–30s each side** — non-negotiable, every session regardless of time
pressure.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Jumps before lifting** — CNS primers on lifting days, full jump session on Sunday
- **Clean pull day (Wed) is heavier** — adjust the rest of that session accordingly
- **Week 12 deload is mandatory** — cut all volumes 50%, maintain intensity
- **Accessories prime Month 3** — heavy RDL and loaded Nordics are building the eccentric base for absolute strength phase
- **Tempo runs stay sub-lactic** — if your legs are burning, slow down
