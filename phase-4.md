# Phase 4: Strength-Speed / Eccentric Emphasis (Weeks 17–20)

**Goal:** Move heavy bars fast. Develop eccentric strength and triple extension. As lifting days get
more specific, general days become very general.
**Stimulus:** Power cleans to daily max, supramaximal eccentrics. CNS load is very high.
**Tempo:** Explosive on concentrics. Full eccentric control — no rushing the descent.
**Frequency:** 3 lifting days (Mon/Wed/Fri) + 1 jump session (Sun)
**Deload:** Week 20

---

## Sunday — Jump Session

**Total window: 40 minutes**

**Low-effort warm-up (20 min):**
Pogos, skips, bounds, submaximal CMJs (60–70%), approach run-throughs, submaximal approach jumps
at 75–80%.

**Max effort (20 min):**
~12–15 full approach jumps at 100% intent.

**Before the max effort block — CNS primer check:**
- 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab)
- If either reads >10% below recent best → skip the max effort block entirely, keep as warm-up only

**Max effort rest:** 90–120 seconds between attempts. Longer rest than earlier phases — intensity is
at its peak. Every attempt counts.

---

## Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

### CNS Primers
*Always first, before any lifting.*

**Standing CMJ × 2 reps — measured via My Jump Lab**
- Max intent every rep, full reset between jumps
- 3 min full rest between jumps
- **If either jump reads >10% below recent best → cut all working sets by 50% today**

---

### Power Clean (Monday / Friday) — Daily Max
*Strong from the Phase 3 clean pull work — now cash it in. Work up in sets of 3 → 2 → 1, adding
load each set. Stop when a rep slows noticeably or technique breaks. Record your top single.*

| Week        | Protocol                                        |
|-------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| 17          | Build: 3-3-2-1-1. Push for Phase 2 best or more |
| 18          | Build: 3-2-1-1-1. Push the top single           |
| 19          | Build: 3-2-1-1. Absolute daily max              |
| 20 (Deload) | Build: 3-3-2. Stop at RPE 8. No daily max       |

---

### Clean Pull (Wednesday) — Supramaximal Carry-Through
*Maintains pull strength and potentiates the Mon/Fri power clean sessions.*

| Week        | Sets × Reps | Load                |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| 17          | 4 × 3       | 116kg (~105% clean) |
| 18          | 5 × 3       | 118kg (~107%)       |
| 19          | 5 × 3       | 121kg (~110%)       |
| 20 (Deload) | 3 × 3       | 105kg               |

---

### Eccentric Split Squat (Safety Bar)
*Rear foot elevated. Safety bar on shoulders — use the rack uprights to assist the concentric on
working sets. The eccentric, not the concentric, is the stimulus.*

**Build-up sets (all weeks):** 3 × 5 at moderate load, normal tempo. Grooves the pattern before
supramaximal work.

**Working sets:** Hands on rack uprights fully assist the concentric. Slow, full eccentric descent.

| Week        | Working Sets × Reps | Load                                  |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------------------------|
| 17          | 3 × 3               | Slightly above normal split squat max |
| 18          | 3 × 3               | Increase load vs Week 17              |
| 19          | 3 × 2               | Push supramaximal — hands assist fully |
| 20 (Deload) | 2 × 3               | Back to comfortable split squat load  |

---

### Rack Accentuated Eccentric (Back Squat — Eccentric Only)
*Supramaximal load. There is no concentric in this movement.*

**Setup:** Set safety bars just below parallel depth. Load the bar to 110–115% of your back squat
max. Un-rack the bar, lower under full control to the safeties, let it settle completely, then step
out and re-rack. Un-rack fresh for each rep. Never bounce off the safeties.

| Week        | Sets × Reps | Load                       |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------------|
| 17          | 4 × 3       | 182.5kg (~110% squat max)  |
| 18          | 4 × 3       | 185kg (~112%)              |
| 19          | 5 × 3       | 187.5kg (~114%)            |
| 20 (Deload) | 3 × 3       | 175kg (~106%)              |

---

### Full ROM Deadlift (from floor)
*Standard deadlift — full range, from the floor. Keeps posterior chain strong and feeds into the
triple extension pattern. Not a max effort — treat as accessory.*

| Week        | Sets × Reps | Notes   |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 17          | 3 × 4       | RPE 7–8 |
| 18          | 3 × 3       | RPE 8   |
| 19          | 3 × 3       | RPE 8–9 |
| 20 (Deload) | 2 × 4       | RPE 6   |

---

### High-Velocity Accessories — 2 Sets Maximum
*Neural fatigue accumulates extremely fast in Phase 4. Do not exceed 2 sets on any of these.*

**High-velocity calf raise:** 2 × 6 — as fast as possible off the ground, full extension at top.

**Drop RDL:** 2 × 5 — drop down fast, drive up explosively. SSC emphasis on the pull.

**Bouncing calf raise:** 2 × 8 — stay on the balls of the feet, minimal ground contact.

---

## General Days (Tue / Thu)

Lifting days are at their most specific — general days must be correspondingly general.

- Bodyweight circuits (no loaded lower body)
- Upper body only: bench, rows, pull-ups
- **No tempo runs** — tendons and joints need the recovery
- **No jump circuits** — save the legs for lifting days
- Optional: light core, hip mobility, static stretching

---

## Warm-Up (Every Session)

**Copenhagen planks: 3 × 20–30s each side** — non-negotiable, every session.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Rack eccentrics: un-rack fresh each rep** — never bounce, never try the concentric
- **2-set cap on velocity accessories** — exceeding this flattens the jump session
- **Power clean daily max: stop when a rep slows** — grinding is not the stimulus
- **Week 20 deload is mandatory** — Phase 5 requires fresh legs for bar speed work
