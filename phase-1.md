# Phase 1: Volume Building (Weeks 5–8)

**Goal:** Build capacity to handle volume. Phase 1 sits at the concentric, low-force end of the
force-velocity curve — the goal is not strength, it's workload capacity. Everything is deliberately
general and moderate so adaptations stack correctly. Rushing intensity now burns stimulus that works
better later.
**Stimulus:** Concentric-focused volume — higher reps, moderate intensity, general bilateral movements.
**Tempo:** Standard controlled tempo (no strict 4-3 requirement — that was Phase 0's job).
**Frequency:** 3 lifting days (Mon/Wed/Fri) + 1 jump session (Sun)
**Deload:** Week 8 (Week 4 of this phase)

---

## Sunday — Jump Session

*Standalone session the day before Monday lift. Sunday gives a buffer without a full rest day
between jump and lift.*

**Total window: 40 minutes**

**Low-effort warm-up (35 min):**
Pogos, skips, bounds, submaximal CMJs (60–70%), approach run-throughs, submaximal approach jumps
at 75–80%.

**Max effort (5 min):**
~3–4 full approach jumps at 100% intent.

**Before the max effort block — CNS primer check:**
- 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab)
- If either reads >10% below recent best → skip the max effort block entirely, keep as warm-up only

**Max effort rest:** 60–90 seconds between attempts. If the pop drops off, stop early.

*You're not trying to set a PR. Practising max intent after a long warm-up. The ratio is deliberately
conservative — tendons are fresh out of Phase 0.*

---

## Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

### CNS Primers
*Always first, before any lifting.*

**Standing CMJ × 2 reps — measured via My Jump Lab**
- Max intent every rep, full reset between jumps
- 3 min full rest between jumps
- **If either jump reads >10% below recent best → cut all working sets by 50% today**

---

### Trap Bar Jump Squat
*Power exercise — always before squats. Load stays light; speed and intent are the stimulus.
Bilateral and general, suits 2-foot jumpers. Primes the transition to power cleans in Phase 2.*

- Load: ~20% of back squat max (~33kg) — fixed across all weeks
- Full triple extension on every rep — explode off the floor, land softly, full reset
- Rest 2–3 min between sets

| Week       | Sets × Reps |
|------------|-------------|
| 5          | 5 × 4       |
| 6          | 5 × 4       |
| 7          | 5 × 4       |
| 8 (Deload) | 3 × 4       |

## Warm-Up (Every Session)

**Copenhagen planks: 3 × 20–30s each side** — non-negotiable, every session regardless of time
pressure.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Jumps before lifting** — CNS primers on lifting days, full jump session on Sunday
- **Sunday is low-specificity volume** — don't chase approach jump PRs in Month 1
- **Week 8 deload is mandatory** — cut all volumes 50%, maintain intensity
- **Intent over load** — every concentric is violent regardless of the weight on the bar
