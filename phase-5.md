# Phase 5: Speed-Strength / Bar Velocity (Weeks 21–24)

**Goal:** Convert strength to speed. Move from strength-speed → speed-strength. Bars move fast
and light — bar speed is the metric, not load.
**Stimulus:** Olympic lifts at 50–70% max, high-velocity squats, plyometric primers for Phase 6.
**Tempo:** Explosive on every rep. If the bar feels slow, you're doing it wrong.
**Frequency:** 3 lifting days (Mon/Wed/Fri) + 1 jump session (Sun)
**Deload:** Week 24

---

## Sunday — Jump Session

**Total window: 40 minutes**

**Low-effort warm-up (20 min):**
Pogos, skips, bounds, submaximal CMJs (60–70%), approach run-throughs, submaximal approach jumps
at 75–80%.

**Max effort (20 min):**
~12–15 full approach jumps at 100% intent.

**Before the max effort block — CNS primer check:**
- 2 measured CMJ reps (My Jump Lab)
- If either reads >10% below recent best → skip the max effort block entirely, keep as warm-up only

**Max effort rest:** 90–120 seconds between attempts. Full rest — every rep is a quality rep.

---

## Lifting Days (Mon / Wed / Fri)

### CNS Primers
*Always first, before any lifting.*

**Standing CMJ × 2 reps — measured via My Jump Lab**
- Max intent every rep, full reset between jumps
- 3 min full rest between jumps
- **If either jump reads >10% below recent best → cut all working sets by 50% today**

### Back Squat — Ascending Primer + High-Velocity Set
*Do NOT max out the primer — it is a warm-up for the velocity set. Do not do both at high
intensity — neural fry risk.*

**Primer (ascending — all weeks):**
Set 1: 5 reps @ ~65% (~107kg) → Set 2: 4 reps @ ~75% (~124kg) → Set 3: 3 reps @ ~85% (~140kg)

**High-velocity set:**

| Week        | Sets × Reps | Load          | Target                       |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|------------------------------|
| 21          | 1 × 5       | 107kg (~65%)  | Move as fast as possible     |
| 22          | 1 × 5       | 110kg (~67%)  | Time the set — note progress |
| 23          | 1 × 5       | 115kg (~70%)  | Bar speed is the measure     |
| 24 (Deload) | 1 × 3       | 100kg         | Comfortable, fast            |

### Plyometric Accessories — Phase 6 Primers
*Keep plyometric volume low — Phase 6 is where it peaks. These prime the movement patterns only.*

**Med ball throws:** 3 sets × 5 per variation — overhead back throw, between-legs forward throw,
overhead scoop throw.

**Step-off box (low box):** Step off, land and hold for 2 seconds. Focus on landing mechanics.
3 × 5.

**Step-up jumps:** Step up and drive off one leg into a jump. Alternating legs. 3 × 5 each leg.

| Week        | Med Ball | Step-off Box | Step-up Jumps |
|-------------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| 21          | 3 × 5    | 3 × 5        | 3 × 5         |
| 22          | 3 × 5    | 3 × 5        | 3 × 5         |
| 23          | 3 × 5    | 3 × 5        | 3 × 5         |
| 24 (Deload) | 2 × 5    | 2 × 5        | 2 × 5         |

## General Days (Tue / Thu)

Most general they've been — specific lifting is at its highest.

- Static flexibility: hamstrings, hip flexors, calves (10–15 min)
- Light bodyweight circuits or core only
- **No jumping on general days** — save reactive capacity for lifting days
- Optional: barefoot 100m × 10, walk back (aerobic maintenance only, not tempo pace)

---

## Warm-Up (Every Session)

**Copenhagen planks: 3 × 20–30s each side** — non-negotiable, every session.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Bar speed is the metric** — if the bar slows down, reduce load
- **Stay at or below 70%** on the Olympic lifts — going heavier kills the purpose
- **Primer is a warm-up** — don't max out at 85%, save it for the velocity set
- **Plyometric accessories are volume-capped** — Phase 6 is where they peak, don't front-load
- **Week 24 deload is mandatory** — entering Phase 6 fresh is the whole point
